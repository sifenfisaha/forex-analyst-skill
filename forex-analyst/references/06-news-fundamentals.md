# 06 — News & Fundamentals

Technicals tell you *where*; fundamentals and scheduled news tell you *when not to be in the water*. For a conservative intraday trader, news is mostly a **risk filter**, not a strategy.

## High-impact ("red-folder") events — know the calendar

Always check an economic calendar (ForexFactory, Investing.com, Myfxbook) for the **traded currencies** before planning an entry. The heavy hitters:

| Event | Currency | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| **Interest rate decisions** (FOMC, ECB, BoE, BoJ, RBA…) | that central bank's currency | The single biggest driver. Massive volatility. |
| **NFP — Non-Farm Payrolls** (first Friday, 8:30 AM ET) | USD | Huge USD volatility; whipsaws are brutal. |
| **CPI / inflation** | that currency | Drives rate expectations → big moves. |
| **FOMC minutes / central-bank speeches** (Powell, Lagarde…) | that currency | Headline-driven spikes. |
| **GDP, Retail Sales, PMI (ISM)** | that currency | Medium-high impact. |
| **Unemployment / jobless claims** | that currency | Medium impact. |

## The conservative news rule (enforce it)

- ❌ **No new entries in the ±15–30 min window around a high-impact release** on the traded currency. Spreads blow out, slippage spikes, stops get wicked then reversed.
- ⚠️ If already in a trade into news: consider **banking partials and moving to break-even** beforehand, or closing if the runner is small.
- ✅ **After** the dust settles (15–30 min post-release), a clean **sweep + MSS** can be excellent — news is often the *manipulation* fuel in AMD. But for the conservative profile, demand confirmation and standard size; don't trade the spike itself.
- 🚫 **Avoid trading the spike/whipsaw** during the release. That's gambling, not a setup.

**In every trade plan, include a news flag:** `News: clear` / `News: CPI in 40 min — stand aside until 15 min after`.

## Fundamental bias (lightweight context for intraday)

You don't need a macro thesis to day-trade, but a one-line awareness of the prevailing driver keeps you from fading a fundamentally-driven trend:
- **Hawkish central bank** (raising rates / hawkish tone) → currency tends to strengthen.
- **Dovish** (cutting / dovish) → currency tends to weaken.
- **Risk-on** (stocks up, good data) → risk currencies (AUD, NZD) firm; safe havens (JPY, CHF, USD) soften.
- **Risk-off** (fear, bad data, geopolitics) → safe havens (USD, JPY, CHF, gold) firm.

If your technical short aligns with a dovish/​weak-currency backdrop, that's tailwind. If it fights a strong hawkish trend, demand more confluence.

## DXY & correlations (sanity check before entry)

The **US Dollar Index (DXY)** is the tide for USD pairs. Use it as a confluence/filter:

- **EURUSD, GBPUSD, AUDUSD, gold (XAUUSD)** are **inversely** correlated to DXY. DXY pushing into resistance/sweeping a high while you're long EURUSD = tailwind (DXY likely to drop → EUR up). DXY breaking up strongly against your EURUSD long = headwind; reconsider.
- **USDJPY, USDCHF, USDCAD** are **positively** correlated to DXY.
- **EURUSD and GBPUSD** move together (positively correlated) — being long both is *doubling* the same USD-down bet, not diversifying. Count correlated positions as **one risk** (see `05-risk-management.md`).
- **Gold (XAUUSD)** trades like an anti-dollar / risk-and-fear asset; watch DXY and real yields.

**Quick check before entry:** *"Does the DXY (or the correlated pair) agree with this direction?"* If the DXY structure contradicts your setup, that's a yellow flag — downgrade or skip.

## Practical news workflow

1. Before planning: glance at the calendar for **both currencies** in the pair (and USD/DXY).
2. Any high-impact event within ~1 hour of the planned entry? → **flag it**, plan to stand aside in the ±15–30 min window.
3. Note the prevailing fundamental lean (hawkish/dovish, risk-on/off) — does the technical agree?
4. Cross-check the **DXY**/correlated pair for confluence.
5. Put the **news flag** in the trade plan output. Always.
