# 02 — Market Structure & Trend Strength

Market structure is *how* price moves: a sequence of swing highs and swing lows. Read it before anything else — it sets your bias and tells you when a trend is intact vs breaking.

## Swing points (the alphabet)

- **Swing High (SH):** a candle high with lower highs on both sides. A local peak.
- **Swing Low (SL):** a candle low with higher lows on both sides. A local trough.
- Mark only **meaningful** swings (clear pivots that the market reacted to). Don't label every micro-wiggle — that's noise.

## The four structural states

| State | Pattern | Bias |
|---|---|---|
| **Bullish** | Higher Highs **+** Higher Lows (HH/HL) | Favor longs (buy discount) |
| **Bearish** | Lower Highs **+** Lower Lows (LH/LL) | Favor shorts (sell premium) |
| **Ranging** | Equal-ish highs & lows, overlapping candles | No directional edge — fade extremes or stand aside |
| **Transition** | A break of the prior pattern (see below) | Bias is shifting — wait for confirmation |

> **Rule:** trade *with* HTF structure. Counter-trend trades are only for clean HTF extremes after a sweep + shift — not for fading a strong trend mid-move.

---

## BOS vs CHoCH vs MSS (know the difference cold)

These three get conflated constantly. Precise definitions:

- **BOS — Break of Structure (continuation).** Price breaks a swing point **in the direction of the existing trend**. Uptrend makes a new HH by breaking the prior high = bullish BOS = trend continues. BOS *confirms* the trend.

- **CHoCH — Change of Character (first warning of reversal).** Price breaks a swing point **against** the prevailing trend — the first sign momentum may be flipping. In an uptrend, breaking the most recent *higher low* = bearish CHoCH. It's a *warning*, not yet a confirmed reversal.

- **MSS — Market Structure Shift (confirmed reversal, with intent).** A CHoCH that happens **with displacement** — a strong, decisive break (often leaving an FVG) right after a liquidity sweep. MSS is the high-quality version you want for entries: *sweep → displacement break of structure → shift confirmed.*

**Practical hierarchy for entries:** liquidity sweep → **MSS** (displacement CHoCH) → enter on the retrace into the FVG/OB. A CHoCH *without* displacement is weaker; a BOS tells you to stay with trend, not reverse.

---

## Internal vs external structure

- **External (major) structure:** the big swing highs/lows visible on the HTF. Defines the overall trend and the dealing range.
- **Internal (minor) structure:** the smaller swings *inside* the current external leg. You use internal CHoCH/MSS on the LTF to time entries, while external structure sets the bias.

Don't let an internal CHoCH convince you the major trend reversed. Internal shift = entry timing. External shift = bias change.

---

## Premium / Discount (equilibrium)

Take the current dealing range (a significant swing low → swing high). Draw a Fibonacci or just the **50% midpoint = equilibrium**.

- Above 50% = **premium** → a good area to **sell** (expensive).
- Below 50% = **discount** → a good area to **buy** (cheap).
- The **OTE (Optimal Trade Entry)** zone sits in the **62%–79%** retracement of the displacement leg — the sweet spot for entries with HTF bias.

> **Discipline:** in a bullish bias, only hunt longs when price is in **discount**. Buying in premium is chasing. Same logic inverted for shorts.

## Trend strength — is the move real or weak?

Gauge momentum so you don't fade a freight train or chase exhaustion:

**Signs of STRENGTH (with-trend continuation likely):**
- **Displacement candles** — large-bodied, decisive candles that *leave FVGs*. Institutions moving with intent.
- Clean, one-sided legs; shallow pullbacks that respect FVGs/OBs.
- Each BOS followed quickly by another. Little overlap.

**Signs of WEAKNESS / exhaustion (reversal/range likely):**
- Overlapping, small-bodied candles; lots of wicks both sides.
- Pullbacks that fully fill prior FVGs and stall.
- Failure to make new highs/lows after reaching obvious liquidity (a sweep that doesn't extend).
- Divergence between correlated pairs / the DXY (see `06-news-fundamentals.md`).

**Use strength to choose your play:**
- Strong with-trend → trade BOS continuations and OB/FVG retraces in the trend direction.
- Weakness at an HTF extreme after a sweep → that's your CRT reversal setup.

## How to read structure off a screenshot (visual checklist)

1. Identify the **highest TF** in the image; mark its last 3–5 major swing highs/lows.
2. Label the sequence: HH/HL or LH/LL → state the bias in one line.
3. Mark the **current dealing range** and its 50% equilibrium → is price premium or discount?
4. Spot the most recent **BOS** (trend intact) or **CHoCH/MSS** (shift) and note where it happened.
5. Note candle character along the last leg: displacement (strong) vs overlapping (weak).
6. Only then move to liquidity (`03`) and CRT (`01`).

If the screenshot has no timeframe label or is too zoomed-in to see real swings, **ask the user** rather than inventing structure.
