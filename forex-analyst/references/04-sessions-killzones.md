# 04 — Sessions, Killzones & Timing

*When* you trade matters as much as *what* you trade. Liquidity, volatility, and clean CRT manipulations cluster in specific windows. Trading outside them is how good setups turn into chop.

> **Time standard:** all times below are **New York time (ET)**, the institutional reference for ICT/CRT. A GMT column is included. **DST caveat:** the US and UK shift clocks on different dates — when in doubt, anchor to **NY local time** and re-check the GMT offset (ET = GMT‑5 in winter / GMT‑4 in summer). Always confirm the user's current session before timing a setup.

## The sessions

| Session | NY time (ET) | GMT (approx) | Character |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Asian (Tokyo)** | 7:00 PM – 4:00 AM | 00:00 – 09:00 | Low volume, tight range. **Builds the liquidity** (Asian range) that London sweeps. |
| **London** | 2:00 AM – 11:00 AM | 07:00 – 16:00 | First big directional move of the day. High volatility. |
| **New York** | 7:00 AM – 4:00 PM | 12:00 – 21:00 | Highest volume; news-driven. NY AM is prime. |
| **London/NY overlap** | 8:00 AM – 11:00 AM | 13:00 – 16:00 | Most liquidity & volatility of the day. |

## The Killzones (high-probability windows)

| Killzone | NY time (ET) | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| **Asian KZ** | 8:00 PM – 12:00 AM | Sets the range/accumulation. Generally **don't trade**, just mark the range. |
| **London KZ** ⭐ | **2:00 AM – 5:00 AM** | The day's first manipulation. Sweeps Asian range / PDH-PDL, then expands. **Primary window.** |
| **NY AM KZ** ⭐ | **7:00 AM – 10:00 AM** (sweet spot ~8:30–10:00) | Sweeps London's move; news-driven displacement. **Primary window.** |
| **NY Lunch** | 12:00 PM – 1:00 PM | Low quality, choppy. **Avoid new entries.** |
| **NY PM KZ** | 1:30 PM – 4:00 PM | Afternoon reversal / continuation. Secondary. |
| **London Close** | 10:00 AM – 12:00 PM | Mean-reversion / profit-taking. Manage runners, scalp reversals. |

**For this user (intraday day-trader): focus on the London KZ and NY AM KZ.** Those two windows produce the cleanest CRT manipulations and the best RR. If a setup won't mature inside one of them, set an alert and wait rather than forcing a low-probability entry.

## The Judas Swing (session-open fakeout)

Near a session/killzone open, price often makes a **false move in the *wrong* direction first** — the "Judas swing" — to grab liquidity before reversing into the real move. Classic examples:
- **London Judas:** spikes to sweep the Asian high (or low), traps breakout traders, then reverses for the real London move.
- **NY Judas:** sweeps the London session high/low at the NY open, then delivers the NY move.

**Application:** at session open, don't chase the first thrust. *Expect* it to be a Judas sweep, wait for the sweep + MSS, then trade the reversal. This IS the CRT manipulation phase, time-anchored.

## Day-of-week tendencies (Quarterly-Theory weekly cycle)

Not laws — *tendencies*. Use as confluence, not signals.

| Day | Tendency |
|---|---|
| **Monday** | Often accumulation / narrow range. Sets the week's initial liquidity. Lower conviction. |
| **Tuesday** | Frequently where the **weekly high or low forms**; strong expansion days. Prime. |
| **Wednesday** | Expansion / continuation of Tuesday's move. Prime. Often the weekly extreme too. |
| **Thursday** | Continuation or reversal; watch for weekly-range completion. |
| **Friday** | Profit-taking, reversals, position-squaring. **Avoid late-Friday entries** (weekend gap risk + thin liquidity). News spikes (NFP first Fri). |

**Intraday implication:** the meat of clean trends is often **Tuesday–Wednesday**. Be more selective Monday and Friday.

---

## Practical timing rules

- ✅ Prioritize setups maturing in the **London KZ** or **NY AM KZ**.
- ✅ Mark the **Asian range** before London; it's the first liquidity target.
- ✅ Expect a **Judas swing** at killzone opens — fade it with confirmation, don't chase it.
- ❌ Avoid new entries during **NY lunch (12–1 PM ET)** and **late Friday**.
- ❌ Don't force a trade just because you're at the screen. **No killzone + no setup = no trade.**
- ⏰ If the user is analyzing outside a killzone for a setup that needs one, say: *"Mark the levels, set an alert for the London/NY KZ, and wait."*
